What is this?

This web page helps you find the 35mm/full-frame-format equivalent to a focal length in a different sensor- or film-format.

Most photographers are familiar with focal lengths in the 35mm/full-frame image format and what field of view that focal length represents (24 mm = wide angle, 50 mm = “normal” etc.). Different formats, however, require different focal length lenses to produce an equivalent field of view.

This site helps you to look up those focal lengths in various imaging formats.

Calculation

The equivalent focal lengths are calculated based on the diagonal of each imaging format.

For example:
35mm diagonal ≈ 43.3 mm.
APS-C diagonal ≈ 28.8 mm

Ratio: 43.3 mm / 28.8 mm ≈ 1.5

23mm in APS-C ≈ 35 mm in 35mm/full-frame format, because 23 mm * 1.5 ≈ 35 mm.
